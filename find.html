How to arrive

Find Us

We're not on Google Maps. We've never been on Google Maps. We don't need to be.

You begin at the corner where three streets have an argument about their names. If you're unsure which corner, you've walked past it. Go back. Turn left at the broken streetlight—the one that's been broken for seven years. We tried to fix it once. The light came back wrong. We left it.

Walk for the amount of time it takes to forget why you started walking. You'll pass a bookstore that may or may not be open. There's no sign. There's never been a sign. If you can see in the window, the bookstore owner is having one of their better days.

When you hear the sound of water—not a fountain, not a river, but the specific sound of someone pouring a kettle at 2 in the morning—you're close. Closer still when you smell coffee and the rain that hasn't fallen yet.

There's a narrow door between a shuttered shop and a wall. The door is dark. The paint has been dark for so long it's forgotten other colors. Push it. It opens. Everything inside is warm.

By Night

The best time to arrive is when you feel like you have nowhere else to be. This is usually between midnight and whenever you decide to leave.

Bring: yourself. A reason helps, but it's not required. We've learned not to ask.

Always Open

By Day

We don't officially open during the day. But if you find the door unlocked, come in. Someone is always here. That someone might be us, or it might be a regular who forgot to leave six hours ago.

We don't turn people away.

When You Need It

Practical Matters

Phone We don't have a phone. We've tried having one. People were uncomfortable with the silence when nobody called.
Email We don't have email either. If you need to reach us, you already know where we are. That's how it works.
Directions We can't give you an address. The street signs keep changing their minds. But if you get lost, keep walking. The rain knows the way.
Parking Park wherever you want. Nobody's watching. Nobody's ever watching.
Cash or Card We accept both. We also accept the faded ticket stub from a concert you went to alone. We once accepted a poem. We framed it.

If you're reading this, you're already close. You just haven't walked far enough yet.

Come find us when you're ready to sit still for a while.

The 2AM Café no wifi. no loud music. no closing time.
