The Regulars

We don't ask names. They gave them anyway. Some of these may be accurate.

Table Two

Margaret

"The Archivist"
Arrives 12:47am, exactly
Orders The Chamomile, never the coffee
Seen with Notebooks. Many. Always a different one.

She writes for three hours without looking up. Once, someone asked what she was writing. She smiled and turned the notebook around. It was blank. She's been coming back for six years.

More

She has never ordered the biscuit, though it always comes. She leaves it on the table untouched. We've stopped taking it away. It feels like it belongs to her now, even if she doesn't want it.

Table Seven

The Professor

"We don't know his real name"
Arrives After the rain starts
Orders The Black, twice, an hour apart
Seen with A newspaper, three weeks old

He argues with the newspaper under his breath. He has been known to fold it triumphantly, as if he has won. He tips generously and once left a note that said only: "you were right about the fog."

More

We have never seen him arrive. He is simply there when the rain begins, as if he and the weather have an arrangement we were not invited to understand.

Counter Seat Three

Lena

"The One Who Always Leaves at 4"
Arrives 1:30am on Thursdays only
Orders Something Warm (lets us decide)
Seen with Headphones. Never plays anything.

She sits at the counter and watches the street. Nobody sits next to her, not because she's unfriendly — she's said hello every time — but because something about her suggests she is waiting for a specific person. They haven't come yet.

More

She once asked us to hold a letter for her. We put it behind the bar. That was eight months ago. She hasn't asked for it back. We haven't opened it.

The Armchair by the Window

Whoever Needs It

"First Come"
Arrives Varies
Orders Anything hot
Seen with A look that says: I just needed to sit down

The armchair by the window has been occupied by hundreds of people who all had the same expression. We don't remember their faces. We remember that they always looked better by the time they left.

More

The cushion has a small burn mark from a candle that fell in 2019. We've never had it fixed. It feels important to keep it exactly as it is. Some things should show their history.

Table Four

————

Table four is always set. The cup is clean. The chair is pulled out slightly, as if someone just stood up. Nobody sits there. Nobody has ever sat there, as far as anyone can remember. We stopped asking. We still set it every night.
