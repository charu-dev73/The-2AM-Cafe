Tonight's Jazz

They play until someone cries. Then they play one more.

They've been playing here since before anyone can remember. Elliot Vane may or may not be a real person. The trumpet player has never spoken. They don't have a manager. They don't have a website. They just show up. Every night. Without fail. The piano sounds like someone remembering something they tried to forget.

Tonight's Setlist

First Set — begins at midnight

01 In the Hours Before It Mattered slow opener
02 Fog on the Window Again played in half-time
03 She Left in March (Reprise) they always play this one twice
04 The Kind of Blue That Isn't Sadness trumpet takes over here
05 Something My Father Used to Hum no one knows the title. we named it.

Second Set — begins at 2am

06 The Night Bus tempo picks up here
07 Nobody Dances Anymore (But We Do) upbeat, unexpected
08 Elliot's Unfinished One ends differently every time
09 Rain, Correctly bass carries this one
10 One More (the encore they never announce) after someone cries

The Band

Piano Elliot Vane or someone using his name
Trumpet Unknown has never spoken. plays like apologies sound.
Double Bass Mara just Mara. been here the longest.
Drums The Young One different person every few months. always fits perfectly.

They don't take requests. They don't take breaks longer than a cigarette. They don't explain what anything means. If you ask, Elliot will look at you for a long moment and then turn back to the piano. His fingers will find the keys like they've been waiting.

That is, we think, the answer.

Come early. Sit by the window. Listen to the tuning. That's not the beginning of the song—it's already started. The song began when you decided to come here.

The 2AM Café no wifi. no loud music. no closing time.
